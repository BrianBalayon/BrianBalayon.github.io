"My 'Dila'"



When I was younger,
I was was told I look more like my mother,
Other times, more like my father.
Had my mother's eyes.
Had my father's nose.
But whose tongue was and still is in my mouth?

Is this the tongue of my mother?
Is this the tongue of my father's mother?

Where did the tongue, which I am using to say these words, come from?
Who decided to rip out my tongue and replace it with this one?

After 18 years, I know now that my tongue had withered away.
After 18 years, that this one is but a proesthetic, serves purpose, lacking feeling.
After 18 years, I know two tongues, learning a third, a still missing my own.

One, English. It's supposedly not the language I first learned.
I first uttered it at fours years old.
And it's what I've uttered ever since.

Dos, Español. Two, Spanish.
It was imposed on me by the education system.
I first uttered it at 14 years old.
Uttered only to order food once in a blue moon.

Sān, Zhōng wén. (三，中文) Three, Chinese.
It was stumbled upon by curiosity to learn
I first uttered it at 18 years old,
And so far I can say, "Wǒ jīnnián xué Zhōngguó wén. (我今年学中文) This year I am learning Chinese.“

Apat, Tagalog. Four Tagalog. It is my mother's tongue and my father's mother's tongue.
I first uttered it when I first began to speak,
But I usually respond "Hindi ko alam ano ang sasabi mo. I do not know what you are saying"

But I want to know what you are saying.
Pero gusto ko alam ano ang sasabi mo.
These tongues weren't meant for me.
Estas lenguas no son para mi.
But this is my tongue,
Pero it ay akin dila,
The tongue of my mother and my father's mother.
Ang dila ng akin nanay at nanay ng akin tatay.
